Overview

Purpose

This site is intended to be a mini-wiki for the Heroes of Might and Magic series, focusing on the history of the landmark franchise. This will sort of model the official and fan wikis, but with added side content from the periphery of the series.

Audience

Primarily fans of the series, but also people interested in the development of the broad video game industry and strategy games.

History

Here I’ll cover the history of the franchise. I’ll start with a brief overview of 3DO and New World Computing, and what they were working on before they started making the Heroes games. I’ll talk about how their experience with their RPG series influenced their design principles when it came to making a game of a completely different genre. The first chunk will talk about how Jon Van Caneghem started making the series, and how his wife talked him into branching out into the heroes series more turn-based strategy type of gameplay. I’ll then talk about how they advertised, sold and distributed their games independently. I’ll have to look it up again but at the start of one of the two series they had to manually take calls and mail the games. It sold way better than expected and the series took off from there Next I’ll briefly cover the second heroes game, before moving on to the third game, where critically speaking the series peaked. I’ll talk about why it’s still played and so heavily referenced in the industry today. I’ll talk about the situation at the developer that led to some of the design choices of IV before they declared bankruptcy and sold the copyright to Ubisoft. I’ll talk about (my favorite) the fifth entry, and how the first entry not developed by the initial developer was handled and received After the fifth game ill talk about 6 and 7 and the diminishing returns they got off them, before covering the series’ hiatus and what ubisoft did with it in the meantime. I’ll cover the upcoming 8th game and how many people are optimistic about what seems to be both a return to form and reimagining of the classic formula. Finally ill discuss how the fans have kept the series alive through mods, namely Horn of the Abyss for III and 5.5 for V. I might also make mention of the spiritual successors like Songs of Conquest and Hero’s Hour.

Music

The music page is going to be devoted to the work of Paul Anthony Romero, who worked on pretty much every game’s soundtrack, both of the heroes series and the rpg series it was based on. I’ll talk about how Mr Romero got his start composing, and what got him into making video game soundtracks. I’ll talk about his timeless contributions to the series, and I might even get an interview with him (work in progress).
